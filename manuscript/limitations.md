# Limitations

This study has several limitations. First, the primary NHANES 2007-2012 analysis was cross-sectional; therefore, the temporal ordering of UACR and TT4 cannot be established. Second, although the UACR-TT4 association was stable across multiple discovery-cohort sensitivity analyses, it was not statistically replicated in NHANES III. Differences in survey era, laboratory methods, population structure, or residual confounding may have contributed to this heterogeneity, but the present analyses cannot distinguish among these explanations.

Third, the discovery cohort used complete-case criteria that required common thyroid indicators and cohort-construction covariates. This approach improved comparability across analyses but may have introduced selection bias. In particular, complete TSH, TT4, TGAb, and TPOAb measurements were required during cohort construction even though TT4 was the manuscript primary thyroid outcome. Complete physical-activity data were also required during cohort construction although physical activity was not included in the final regression adjustment set. These design choices should be considered when interpreting generalizability.

Fourth, mortality analyses were observational. Residual and unmeasured confounding may remain despite adjustment for demographic, socioeconomic, lifestyle, metabolic, kidney-function, and iodine-related covariates. Cardiovascular mortality analyses included fewer events than all-cause analyses, limiting precision. The public-use linked mortality file also provides grouped underlying-cause categories rather than detailed adjudicated cardiovascular outcomes. Joint UACR-TT4 categories did not show a monotonic risk pattern, and multiplicative interaction tests were non-significant.

Finally, the bidirectional MR module was exploratory and is intended for the Supplementary Materials. FT4 and TT4 GWAS were unavailable in the searchable OpenGWAS index, preventing direct genetic evaluation of the UACR-TT4 association. UACR and albuminuria each had only one LD-clumped genome-wide significant instrument. The TSH -> eGFR result was also based on a single SNP and a TSH protein proxy. The MR findings therefore do not establish causality.
