# Discussion outline

## 1. Central finding

- Open with the bounded discovery claim: higher UACR was associated with higher TT4 in NHANES 2007-2012.
- Emphasize robustness: the continuous association remained positive across prespecified sensitivity analyses and the spline analysis did not suggest non-linearity.
- Keep the verb observational: use "was associated with", not "increased", "caused", or "led to".

## 2. Why the TT4 finding may matter

- Frame TT4 as a marker of thyroid-hormone homeostasis rather than proof of a thyroid disease mechanism.
- Note that kidney dysfunction, albumin handling, iodine exposure, metabolic factors, and assay-era differences could influence observed associations.
- Treat these as plausible explanations for future investigation, not mechanisms demonstrated by this study.

## 3. NHANES III difference

- State plainly that the association was not statistically replicated in NHANES III.
- Report that harmonized NHANES III estimates were close to zero and that the cohort-interaction test supported heterogeneity.
- Discuss possible sources of heterogeneity: survey era, laboratory methods, population structure, covariate measurement, and residual confounding.
- Avoid presenting NHANES III as a successful replication.

## 4. Mortality relevance

- Separate the strong UACR mortality signal from the more modest TT4 signal.
- UACR was consistently associated with all-cause and cardiovascular mortality.
- TT4 was associated with all-cause mortality but not cardiovascular mortality and should be described as a secondary prognostic marker.
- Joint UACR-TT4 categories were not monotonic and interaction tests were non-significant. Do not claim a mortality interaction or combined-risk gradient.

## 5. Supplementary exploratory MR

- Keep MR outside the main argument.
- Explain that direct genetic evaluation of UACR -> TT4 was unavailable because TT4 GWAS were not identified in the searchable OpenGWAS index.
- Note that UACR and albuminuria analyses were limited by single-SNP instruments.
- Label TSH -> eGFR as a single-SNP exploratory result based on a TSH protein proxy.
- State that the MR findings do not establish causality.

## 6. Strengths

- Nationally sampled NHANES discovery cohort with complex-survey methods.
- Cycle-specific pooled weighting strategy for thyroid-related analyses.
- Multiple exposure definitions, RCS modeling, and prespecified TT4 sensitivity analyses.
- Separate NHANES III replication assessment with harmonized models.
- Prospective public-use mortality linkage with all-cause and cardiovascular outcomes.
- Reproducible Python and R workflow with retained logs and derived tables.

## 7. Limitations

- Cross-sectional UACR-TT4 analysis cannot establish temporality.
- NHANES III did not statistically reproduce the discovery association.
- Selection may be affected by complete-case requirements for common thyroid indicators and cohort-construction covariates.
- Mortality analyses remain observational and may be affected by residual confounding.
- Cardiovascular mortality event counts were smaller than all-cause event counts.
- The public-use mortality file uses grouped underlying-cause categories.
- Exploratory MR could not directly test TT4 and was instrument-limited.

## 8. Closing paragraph

- Conclude that UACR was associated with TT4 in NHANES 2007-2012 and with subsequent mortality.
- State that TT4 may represent a secondary marker warranting further study.
- End with the boundary: the TT4 association was not statistically replicated in NHANES III, and the current MR analysis does not establish causality.
